# Engineering Writing Workflow

WriteLikeMe treats writing as a repeatable production system. The workspace starts from a URL or pasted text, turns it into research and decision context, defines what the reader should gain, selects a structure, then drafts each section before adapting the result to platforms.

## Core Principle

The article should close the gap between the reader's current cognition and the article's unique value.

That gap is the writing objective. It is not only "what topic to write about", but what the reader can understand, do, feel, or decide after reading.

## Objective System

The workflow uses a `Reader Value Objective` system inspired by backward design and three-dimensional learning objectives, adapted for professional writing.

### 1. Knowledge And Judgment

What the reader should newly understand or distinguish.

Examples:

- Understand the difference between an AI writing tool and a marketing execution workspace.
- See why "more drafts" is not the same as "better publishing workflow".
- Recognize the strategic signal behind a product, market, repo, or news event.

### 2. Method And Process

What the reader can now apply.

Examples:

- Turn one source into user research, demand map, angle, outline, and platform assets.
- Evaluate whether a topic is worth writing before drafting.
- Use a structure template to make the article easier to follow.

### 3. Emotion, Attitude, And Decision

What stance, confidence, motivation, or action the reader should leave with.

Examples:

- Feel that content can be engineered without flattening personal voice.
- Decide whether the topic deserves a full article or only a short post.
- Trust the author because the article makes a clear judgment and shows the working path.

## Writing Objective Output

Each run should produce:

- Current cognition: what the target reader likely already knows.
- Unique value: what this article adds.
- Cognitive gap: the precise distance the article must close.
- Three objective dimensions: knowledge/judgment, method/process, attitude/decision.
- Success metrics: evidence that the article achieved its job.

## Structure Design

After the objective is clear, the workflow chooses a structure. The structure must serve the objective, not the other way around.

### Classic Structure Templates

1. Problem, Insight, System, Proof, Action
   Best for analytical product essays and founder writing.

2. Before, Shift, After, Playbook
   Best for explaining market transitions and new workflows.

3. Misconception, Reality, Mechanism, Implication
   Best for contrarian or debate-driven pieces.

4. Case, Pattern, Principle, Checklist
   Best for practical tutorials and operator notes.

5. Signal, Meaning, Bet, Execution
   Best for news-driven trend interpretation.

## Section Writing

Each section should have:

- Section title.
- Section job: what this part must accomplish.
- Reader gain: what the reader obtains in this section.
- Evidence prompt: what proof, quote, source, or example should be added.
- Draft paragraph: an editable first version written in the author's voice.

## Full Workflow

1. Start: capture URL, title, audience, source text, and product context.
2. News: aggregate and incrementally refresh trend/news signals.
3. Insight: compress input and news into core signals.
4. Research: build user persona, knowledge base, demand map, and search leads.
5. Goal: define the Reader Value Objective.
6. Structure: select the best writing structure and section plan.
7. Meeting: evaluate whether the selected topic and structure are worth writing.
8. Voice: apply the author's style fingerprint.
9. Write: draft every section, then generate X thread variants and platform assets.
10. Review: score, optimize, export, and save the run.

## Engineering Notes

- Settings are global configuration and must not occupy a workflow step.
- The workflow should remain waterfall-like: each step produces visible output and a clear action to continue.
- Every generated draft should make its assumptions visible so the writer can confirm or revise.
- The export should include objectives, structure, section drafts, topic evaluation, platform assets, and review scores.
